Introduction

Tables of Contents in Geodesy (TCG) provides tables of contents for selected journals in geodesy. The contents were originally prepared for personal use and are now being made available via the Internet to anyone who wants them. TCG was created by Mike & Mary Craymer and is maintained by them in their spare time.

TCG has also been endorsed by the International Association of Geodesy (see Journal of Geodesy, Vol.70, No.4, pp.241-242) and is used to assist in the compilation of their own bibliographic review published in the IAG Newsletter in the Journal of Geodesy.

Generally, only technical articles from journal issues with at least one article in English are listed in the contents. For multi-lingual issues, all articles will be listed, but in the language of publication (without accents and special characters).

The contents to the current issue of each journal is distributed as soon as it is received by us. When possible, contents to issues announced, but not received, will also be posted to the WWW archive (but not distributed via email until received).

Please note that TCG has no affiliation with the publishers of the journals it covers. It does not distribute any of the articles nor any jounals. See below for different ways of getting reprints of articles.

Online Sources of TCG and Subscriptions

Tables of Contents in Geodesy is available from the following Internet sources:

  • Email mailing list subscription via the Canadian Space Geodesy Forum (CANSPACE) under the topic identifier TCG. To subscribe, send email to listserv@unb.ca with the message (do include any other text, including signatures, in the body of the message)
    SUBSCRIBE CANSPACE your name
    By default you will receive all CANSPACE postings, including TCG. To receive only TCG postings, send another email to the same address with the message
    SET CANSPACE TOPICS: TCG
    To stop receiving TCG postings, send the message:
    SET CANSPACE TOPICS: -TCG
    To unsubscribe from CANSPACE, send the message:
    SIGNOFF CANSPACE
    For more information, see the CANSPACE web page.

  • World Wide Web (this page) at one of the following addresses:

    http://www.geodetic.org/tcg/
    http://www.craymer.com/tcg/

  • FTP access is once again supported at the following address:

    ftp://gauss.geod.nrcan.gc.ca/pub/GSD/craymer/tcg/

The WWW site archives all of the distributed contents.

Reprints of Articles

Articles in the tables of contents can be obtained in different ways, including:

  • Visit a library that carries the particular journal you want.

  • Order reprints or issues directly from the publisher. The address of each publisher (including email and WWW if available) is given at the bottom of every tables of contents.

  • Order a copy of the article through your local library via inter-library loan from the Earth Sciences Information Centre, Natural Resources Canada. Document delivery services are also available. For more information contact:
    Earth Sciences Information Centre
    Att: Inter-Library Loans & Document Delivery
    Natural Resources Canada
    601 Booth Street, Ottawa K1A 0E8, Canada
    Tel. 613-996-1604, Fax. 613-943-8742
    Email: esic.ill@nrcan.gc.ca
    WWW: http://www.nrcan.gc.ca/ess/esic/esic_e.html

  • Order a copy of the article direct from the Canadian Institute for Scientific and Technical Information (CISTI), a world-wide document delivery service. For more information or to place an order, contact:
    Client Assistant, Document Delivery
    CISTI, National Research Council Canada
    Ottawa K1A 0S2, Canada
    Tel. 613-993-9251, Fax. 613-952-8243
    Email: cisti.docdel@nrc.ca
    WWW: http://www.cisti.nrc.ca/cisti/

Note: Copyright regulations require that copies furnished be used solely for private study or research.
